Why The Secret History by Donna Tartt is a GREAT Book

(I'm not done with it yet) - Emily Zhao

The Secret History, debut novel of Donna Tartt, was published in 1992 and is about the mysterious antics between an elite and selective group of college students. Set in remote Vermont, the main character, Richard Papen, attends Hampton College and finds himself intangled between a preexisting clique of students studying Greek.

It's a very autumnal read and I highly suggest you light one too many candles while reading to enjoy the book to the absolute maxiumum. If you want to go a step further, spin a Smiths (or some other pompous band) vinyl on an Audio Technica turntable and drink a scourching cup of BLACK COFFEE because reading this book makes you feel like you're better than everyone else. Speaking of candles, I have a "Spiced Apple Toddy" candle that I bought from Bath and Body Works around 3 years ago and I finally cracked it open and placed it on my windowsill. It smells great.

All in all, The Secret History is great because it immerses you in a highly academic setting but never bores you. Some might disagree (namely Ms. Hall) but their opinions don't matter because Donna Tartt is a PULITZER-PRIZE-WINNING-AUTHOR and she's pretty good at her craft. Then again, she won that Pultizer for a different book...but nonetheless, she's awesome and very skilled.
